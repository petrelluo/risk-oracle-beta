Step 1: The Cast of Characters (Chemical Formulas)

Before writing a sentence, you need to know the words. In chemistry, the "words" are chemical formulas.

  • Symbols: 1- or 2-letter symbols from the Periodic Table (e.g., H for Hydrogen, Na for Sodium).
  • Subscripts (The small, low numbers): These tell you exactly how many atoms of that specific element are glued together in a single molecule. Example: In H2O, the subscript 2 means there are 2 Hydrogen atoms bonded to 1 Oxygen atom.
CRITICAL RULE: Charge Neutrality

When you are constructing the formula for an ionic compound (a metal + a nonmetal), you must ensure the molecule has a net charge of zero. You do this by adjusting the subscripts, NOT the coefficients.

Example: Magnesium is Mg2+. Chlorine is Cl-. To make them neutral, you need two negative Chlorines to balance the positive two of Magnesium. Therefore, the correct formula is MgCl2.

Step 2: The Stage Directions (State Symbols)

Chemicals behave differently depending on their physical state. We indicate this using small letters in parentheses right after the chemical formula:

  • (s) = Solid (like ice, metals, or salt crystals)
  • (l) = Liquid (pure liquids like liquid water or liquid bromine)
  • (g) = Gas (like steam, oxygen, or carbon dioxide)
  • (aq) = Aqueous (meaning the chemical is dissolved in water, like saltwater)

Step 3: Writing the "Skeleton" Equation

A chemical equation is divided into two halves, separated by an arrow.

  • Reactants (Left Side): Your starting ingredients. Separate multiple reactants with a plus sign (+).
  • The Arrow (→): Means "yields" or "reacts to produce".
  • Products (Right Side): The final result of the reaction.

Skeleton Equation Example (Unbalanced): H2(g) + O2(g) → H2O(l)

Step 4: The Law of Conservation (Balancing)

You cannot create or destroy matter. If you start a reaction with 2 oxygen atoms, you MUST end with 2 oxygen atoms.

  • Coefficients (The big numbers in front): These tell you how many entire molecules you have. This is the ONLY number you are allowed to change to balance an equation.
  • If you place a 2 in front of water (2H2O), it multiplies everything inside the molecule by 2 (giving you 4 Hydrogens and 2 Oxygens).
