1. Binary Compounds (Suffix: -ide)

Used for compounds with only two elements. Nonmetal endings change to -ide.

TypeStructureExample
IonicMetal + NonmetalNaCl: Sodium chloride
CovalentPrefix + NonmetalCO₂: Carbon dioxide
Binary AcidHydro-___-ic AcidHCl: Hydrochloric acid

2. The Polyatomic "Ladder" (per-, -ate, -ite, hypo-)

Based on the number of oxygen atoms relative to the standard -ate ion.

Prefix/SuffixOxygen ContextExample (Chlorine)
per-...-ate1 More Oxygen than -ateClO₄⁻: Perchlorate
...-ateThe Standard/Common IonClO₃⁻: Chlorate
...-ite1 Less Oxygen than -ateClO₂⁻: Chlorite
hypo-...-ite2 Less Oxygen than -ateClO⁻: Hypochlorite

3. Oxyacid Transitions

When polyatomic ions form acids, suffixes change, but prefixes like per- and hypo- stay the same.

Ion SuffixAcid SuffixExample
-ate-ic acidNitrate ➔ Nitric acid
-ite-ous acidNitrite ➔ Nitrous acid
per-...-ateper-...-ic acidPerchlorate ➔ Perchloric acid
hypo-...-itehypo-...-ous acidHypochlorite ➔ Hypochlorous acid
