Percentage Change — Step by Step

Real-life money examples. Same 3 steps every single time — no surprises.

The Formula
(New − Old) ÷ Old × 100

1
💰 Savings Grew
You had $200. Now you have $250. By how much did it grow?
1
Find the change
$250 − $200 = $50
2
Divide by original
$50 ÷ $200 = 0.25
3
× 100
0.25 × 100 = 25%
✅ Savings grew by 25%
2
📉 Stock Dropped
You bought a stock at $80. It's now $60. How much did it drop?
1
Find the change
$60 − $80 = −$20
2
Divide by original
−$20 ÷ $80 = −0.25
3
× 100
−0.25 × 100 = −25%
📉 Stock dropped by 25%
3
🏠 Rent Went Up
Rent was $1,200. Raised to $1,350. How big an increase is that?
1
Find the change
$1,350 − $1,200 = $150
2
Divide by original
$150 ÷ $1,200 = 0.125
3
× 100
0.125 × 100 = 12.5%
😬 Rent jumped by 12.5% — significant!
4
💼 Investment Return
You invested $5,000. Two years later it's $6,200. How well did it do?
1
Find the change
$6,200 − $5,000 = $1,200
2
Divide by original
$1,200 ÷ $5,000 = 0.24
3
× 100
0.24 × 100 = 24%
🎉 Investment grew by 24% — solid return!
⚠️ Watch Out — This Trips Everyone Up!

A 50% drop is NOT canceled by a 50% gain. The gain is calculated on the new smaller amount, not the original.

$100 → drops 50% → $50 → gains 50% → only $75 😲  (still down $25!)

Practice 1
A house was worth $300,000. Now it's $360,000. By what percent did it increase?
✅ +20% increase $360k − $300k = $60k $60k ÷ $300k × 100 = 20%
Practice 2
A stock dropped from $500 to $350. What is the percentage drop?
✅ −30% drop $350 − $500 = −$150 −$150 ÷ $500 × 100 = −30%
Practice 3
After a 20% raise, your salary is $60,000. What was it before?
✅ $50,000 original salary $60,000 ÷ (1 + 0.20) $60,000 ÷ 1.20 = $50,000
Practice 4 — Challenge!
Friend A: $1,000→$1,200. Friend B: $4,000→$4,600. Who got the better % return?
