8.1 Right Triangle Trigonometry

Six Trig Ratios (SOH-CAH-TOA)

sin θ = opp/hyp   csc θ = hyp/opp
cos θ = adj/hyp   sec θ = hyp/adj
tan θ = opp/adj   cot θ = adj/opp

Pythagorean: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

30°–60°–90° Triangle

60°√3/21/2√3

Solving angles: θ = sin⁻¹, cos⁻¹, or tan⁻¹

Examples

Ex 1 — Right triangle: legs 5 and 12; find all 6 ratios for the angle opposite the leg of 5.

hyp = √(25+144) = 13; opp = 5, adj = 12

sin θ = 5/13, cos θ = 12/13, tan θ = 5/12

csc θ = 13/5, sec θ = 13/12, cot θ = 12/5

Ex 2 — Find the side x: right triangle, hyp = 10, one angle = 30°, x is opposite to 30°.

sin 30° = x/10 → x = 10·sin 30° = 10·(1/2) = 5

Ex 3 — Find angle θ: right triangle, opp = 8, adj = 6.

tan θ = 8/6 = 4/3 → θ = tan⁻¹(4/3) ≈ 53.13°

Ex 4 — Angle of elevation: from 80 ft away, the top of a building has angle of elevation 40°. Find the height.

tan 40° = h/80 → h = 80·tan 40° ≈ 67.1 ft

8.2 Angles and Radian Measure

Degrees ↔ Radians

radians = degrees · π/180
degrees = radians · 180/π

Coterminal angles: add or subtract multiples of 360° (or 2π)

Reference angle: acute angle between terminal side and x-axis

Arc Length & Sector Area

Arc length:   s = rθ   (θ in radians)
Sector area:   A = (1/2)r²θ   (θ in radians)

Always convert θ to radians before using these formulas.

Examples

Ex 5 — Convert 135° to radians.

135 · π/180 = 135π/180 = 3π/4

Ex 6 — Convert 7π/6 to degrees.

(7π/6) · 180/π = 7·180/6 = 210°

Ex 7 — Find two coterminal angles for −50°.

Positive: −50 + 360 = 310°

Negative: −50 − 360 = −410°

Ex 8 — Arc length: r = 6, θ = 2π/3.

s = 6·(2π/3) = 4π ≈ 12.57

8.4 Graphing Sine and Cosine Functions

General Form

y = a sin(bx − h) + k    y = a cos(bx − h) + k
FeatureFormula
Amplitude|a|
Period2π/|b|
Phase shifth/b (right if positive)
Midliney = k
Max/Mink ± |a|

Key Cycle Points

For y = a sin(bx − h) + k, one full cycle starts at x = h/b:

  • Start: (h/b, k)
  • Peak: (h/b + T/4, k + |a|)
  • Middle: (h/b + T/2, k)
  • Trough: (h/b + 3T/4, k − |a|)
  • End: (h/b + T, k)

where T = period = 2π/|b|

Examples

Ex 13 — Amplitude & period of y = −3sin(2x).

a = −3 → Amplitude = |−3| = 3

b = 2 → Period = 2π/2 = π

Ex 14 — Features of y = 2cos(x − π/3) + 1.

Amplitude: 2  |  Period: 2π

Phase shift: π/3 (right)  |  Midline: y = 1

Max: 3 at x = π/3  |  Min: −1 at x = 4π/3

Ex 15 — Write a sine equation: amplitude 4, period π, phase shift π/4 right, midline y = −2.

b = 2π/π = 2; h = π/4·b = π/2; k = −2

y = 4sin(2x − π/2) − 2

Ex 16 — Identify features of y = (1/2)cos(πx/2 + π/4).

a = 1/2; b = π/2; h = −π/4

Amplitude: 1/2  |  Period: 2π/(π/2) = 4

Phase shift: (−π/4)/(π/2) = −1/2 (left 1/2)

8.5 Graphing Other Trigonometric Functions

Tangent & Cotangent

y = a tan(bx − h) + k

Period = π/|b|  |  No amplitude

Vertical asymptotes where cos = 0: x = π/(2b) + h/b + nπ/b

y = a cot(bx − h) + k

Period = π/|b|  |  Asymptotes where sin = 0

Secant & Cosecant

Reciprocal functions — graph by drawing the base first:

sec x = 1/cos x  → graph from y = cos x

csc x = 1/sin x  → graph from y = sin x

Key: Where cos = 0 → V. asymptote of sec; where sin = 0 → V. asymptote of csc

Period of sec and csc = 2π/|b|

y = tan(x)

y = sec(x) & y = cos(x)

Examples

Ex 17 — Period and asymptotes of y = 2tan(3x).

Period = π/3

Asymptotes: 3x = π/2 + nπ → x = π/6 + nπ/3

Ex 18 — Period and asymptotes of y = csc(x/2).

Based on y = sin(x/2); Period = 2π/(1/2) = 4π

Asymptotes: x/2 = nπ → x = 2nπ  (n = 0, ±1, ±2, …)

Ex 19 — Features of y = −sec(2x) + 1.

Based on y = cos(2x): amplitude n/a, period π

Asymptotes: 2x = π/2 + nπ → x = π/4 + nπ/2

Midline: y = 1; branches go to y ≤ 0 and y ≥ 2

Ex 20 — Period and phase shift of y = cot(2x − π/2).

b = 2; h = π/2; Period = π/2

Phase shift = h/b = (π/2)/2 = π/4 (right)

8.6 Modeling with Trigonometric Functions

Writing Sinusoidal Models

Given max M and min m of a sinusoidal data set:

Amplitude = (M − m)/2
Midline k = (M + m)/2
Period T from data; then b = 2π/T

Choose sin or cos based on starting position; adjust h for phase shift.

Strategy for Real-World Models

  1. Identify max, min → compute amplitude and midline
  2. Identify period from data (time for one full cycle)
  3. Write y = a sin(bx − h) + k or cosine version
  4. Adjust phase shift h to match the starting point
  5. Verify with given data points

Examples

Ex 21 — Temperature: high 88°F, low 54°F; period 365 days; max on day 200. Write a model.

Amplitude = (88−54)/2 = 17  |  Midline k = (88+54)/2 = 71

b = 2π/365; max at day 200 → phase shift: h = 200·b = 400π/365

y = 17sin(2πt/365 − 400π/365) + 71

Ex 22 — Write an equation for a graph: max 5, min −1, period 4π, starts at midline going up at x = 0.

a = (5−(−1))/2 = 3; k = (5+(−1))/2 = 2

b = 2π/(4π) = 1/2; starts at midline going up → sine, h = 0

y = 3sin(x/2) + 2
