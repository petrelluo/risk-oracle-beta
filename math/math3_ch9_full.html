9.1 Using Trigonometric Identities

Pythagorean Identities

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
1 + cot²θ = csc²θ

Rearrangements: sin²θ=1−cos²θ; tan²θ=sec²θ−1; cot²θ=csc²θ−1

Reciprocal & Quotient

csc θ=1/sinθ   secθ=1/cosθ   cotθ=1/tanθ
tanθ=sinθ/cosθ   cotθ=cosθ/sinθ

Even / Odd

cos(−θ)=cosθ, sec(−θ)=secθ (even)
sin(−θ)=−sinθ, tan(−θ)=−tanθ (odd)

Co-function & Verification Strategy

sin(π/2−θ)=cosθ   cos(π/2−θ)=sinθ   tan(π/2−θ)=cotθ

To verify an identity:

  1. Work on the more complex side only
  2. Convert to sin and cos
  3. Factor or use Pythagorean subs.
  4. Multiply by a conjugate if needed

Examples

Ex 1 — Simplify (1−cos²θ)/sinθ

= sin²θ/sinθ = sinθ

Ex 2 — Given cosθ=−3/5 (Q III), find sinθ and tanθ.

sin²θ=1−9/25=16/25; sinθ=−4/5 (Q III)

tanθ=(−4/5)/(−3/5)=4/3

Ex 3 — Verify: (tanθ+cotθ)sinθcosθ=1

LHS = (sinθ/cosθ+cosθ/sinθ)sinθcosθ = (sin²θ+cos²θ) = 1 ✓

Ex 4 — Verify: secx−sinx·tanx=cosx

LHS = 1/cosx − sinx·(sinx/cosx) = (1−sin²x)/cosx = cos²x/cosx = cosx ✓

9.2 Using Sum and Difference Formulas

Sum & Difference

sin(A±B) = sinA cosB ± cosA sinB
cos(A±B) = cosA cosB ∓ sinA sinB
tan(A±B) = (tanA±tanB)/(1∓tanA tanB)

Double-Angle & Half-Angle

sin(2θ) = 2sinθcosθ
cos(2θ) = cos²θ−sin²θ = 1−2sin²θ = 2cos²θ−1
sin(θ/2) = ±√((1−cosθ)/2)
cos(θ/2) = ±√((1+cosθ)/2)

Examples

Ex 5 — Find sin 75° exactly.

sin(45°+30°)=(√2/2)(√3/2)+(√2/2)(1/2)=(√6+√2)/4

Ex 6 — Given sinθ=3/5 (Q II), find sin(2θ) and cos(2θ).

cosθ=−4/5; sin(2θ)=2(3/5)(−4/5)=−24/25

cos(2θ)=1−2(9/25)=7/25

Ex 7 — Find cos(π/8) using half-angle.

cos(π/8)=√((1+cos(π/4))/2)=√((2+√2)/4)=√(2+√2)/2

Ex 8 — Simplify cos(x+π).

cosxcosπ−sinxsinπ = cosx(−1)−sinx(0) = −cosx

9.3 Law of Sines

Law of Sines

a/sinA = b/sinB = c/sinC

Use when given: AAS, ASA, or SSA (ambiguous case)

Area formula:

Area = (1/2)bc sinA = (1/2)ac sinB = (1/2)ab sinC

Ambiguous Case (SSA)

Given angle A and sides a, b:

ConditionTriangles
a < b sinA0 (no triangle)
a = b sinA1 (right triangle)
b sinA < a < b2 triangles
a ≥ b1 triangle

Examples

Ex 9 — AAS: A=40°, B=75°, a=12. Find b and c.

C=180−40−75=65°

b/sin75°=12/sin40°; b=12sin75°/sin40°≈18.03

c=12sin65°/sin40°≈16.91

Ex 10 — Find area: b=9, c=14, A=30°.

Area=(1/2)(9)(14)sin30°=(1/2)(9)(14)(0.5)=31.5 sq units

Ex 11 — SSA ambiguous case: A=30°, a=6, b=10.

b sinA=10sin30°=5; 5<6<10 → two triangles

sinB₁=bsinA/a=10(0.5)/6=5/6; B₁≈56.4°; B₂≈123.6°

Ex 12 — Two ships leave port; one travels N30°E for 20 mi, other N70°E for 15 mi. Find the distance between them.

Angle between them=40°; apply Law of Cosines (see 9.4)

9.4 Law of Cosines

Law of Cosines

a² = b² + c² − 2bc cosA
b² = a² + c² − 2ac cosB
c² = a² + b² − 2ab cosC

Solving for an angle: cosA = (b²+c²−a²)/(2bc)

Use when given: SAS or SSS

Heron's Formula (area from SSS)

s = (a+b+c)/2   (semi-perimeter)
Area = √(s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c))

When to use which law:

GivenUse
AAS, ASA, SSALaw of Sines
SAS, SSSLaw of Cosines

Examples

Ex 13 — SAS: b=12, c=9, A=50°. Find a.

a²=144+81−2(12)(9)cos50°=225−216(0.643)≈225−138.9=86.1

a≈9.28

Ex 14 — SSS: a=8, b=11, c=6. Find angle B.

cosB=(64+36−121)/(2·8·6)=−21/96=−0.219

B≈102.6°

Ex 15 — Heron's Formula: a=7, b=9, c=5. Find area.

s=(7+9+5)/2=10.5

Area=√(10.5·3.5·1.5·5.5)=√(302.8125)≈17.4 sq units

Ex 16 — Two ships from port: one travels 30 mi E, the other 25 mi at N60°E. Find distance between them.

Angle between = 30°; c²=900+625−2(30)(25)cos30°=1525−1299≈226

c≈15 mi
