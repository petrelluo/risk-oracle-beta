6.1 Inverse Variation

Key Concept

y varies inversely with x when their product is constant:

y = k/x   or   xy = k   (k ≠ 0)

k is the constant of variation. Find k by computing xy from any known point.

If (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) are on the same curve:

x₁y₁ = x₂y₂

Direct vs. Inverse Variation

DirectInverse
Equationy = kxy = k/x
Testy/x = kxy = k
GraphLine through originHyperbola
AsymptotesNonex = 0, y = 0

k > 0 → branches in Q I & Q III  |  k < 0 → Q II & Q IV

Examples

Ex 1 — Write the inverse variation equation: y = 8 when x = 5

k = xy = 5 · 8 = 40

Equation: y = 40/x

Ex 2 — y varies inversely with x. Given (4, 15), find y when x = 12.

x₁y₁ = x₂y₂ → 4(15) = 12 · y₂

60 = 12y₂ → y₂ = 5

Ex 3 — Does the table show inverse variation? x: 2, 4, 6   y: 9, 4.5, 3

xy: 2·9=18, 4·4.5=18, 6·3=18 — product is constant

Yes, inverse variation with k = 18

Ex 4 — The time t to drive varies inversely with speed s. At 60 mph: 2.5 h. Find t at 75 mph.

k = 60(2.5) = 150;   t = 150/75 = 2 hours

6.2 Graphing Rational Functions

Simple Form: f(x) = a/(x − h) + k

Vertical asymptote: x = h
Horizontal asymptote: y = k

Domain: all reals except x = h

Range: all reals except y = k

a > 0 → branches in Q I & Q III relative to (h, k)

a < 0 → branches in Q II & Q IV relative to (h, k)

Rewrite (ax+b)/(cx+d) by long division to get this form.

General Form: f(x) = p(x)/q(x)

Vertical asymptotes: zeros of q(x) after simplifying

Holes: x-values of common factors that cancel

Horizontal asymptote:

  • deg(p) < deg(q):   y = 0
  • deg(p) = deg(q):   y = leading coefficient ratio
  • deg(p) > deg(q):   no H. asymptote

x-intercepts: zeros of numerator (after simplifying)

y-intercept: f(0), if defined

Examples

Ex 5 — Asymptotes and domain/range of f(x) = 3/(x + 2) − 1

h = −2, k = −1

V. asymptote: x = −2  |  H. asymptote: y = −1

Domain: x ≠ −2  |  Range: y ≠ −1

Ex 6 — Rewrite (2x + 7)/(x + 3) in the form a/(x − h) + k

Divide: 2x+7 ÷ (x+3) → quotient 2, remainder 1

f(x) = 2 + 1/(x + 3) = 1/(x + 3) + 2

V. asymptote: x = −3  |  H. asymptote: y = 2

Ex 7 — Asymptotes and holes of h(x) = (x² − 4)/(x² − x − 6)

Factor: (x−2)(x+2)/[(x−3)(x+2)]

Cancel (x+2): Hole at x = −2

V. asymptote: x = 3  |  H. asymptote: y = 1 (equal degrees)

Ex 8 — Find all key features of f(x) = (3x − 6)/(x² − 4x + 4)

Factor: 3(x−2)/(x−2)² = 3/(x−2)

V. asymptote: x = 2  |  H. asymptote: y = 0  |  No hole (x−2 fully cancels)

x-intercept: none  |  y-intercept: f(0) = −3/2

6.3 Multiplying and Dividing Rational Expressions

Simplify

ac/bc = a/b  (c ≠ 0)

Factor numerator and denominator, then cancel common factors.

Always state excluded values.

Multiply

(a/b) · (c/d) = ac/bd

Factor all expressions first, cancel across any numerator/denominator, then multiply.

Divide

(a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) · (d/c)

Multiply by the reciprocal of the divisor, then simplify as multiplication.

Examples

Ex 9 — Simplify: (3x² − 12x)/(x² − 16)

Factor: 3x(x−4)/[(x+4)(x−4)]

Cancel (x−4): 3x/(x+4),   x ≠ 4, x ≠ −4

Ex 10 — Multiply: [(x² − 9)/(2x)] · [6x²/(x + 3)]

= (x−3)(x+3)/(2x) · 6x²/(x+3)

Cancel (x+3) and 2x: 3x(x−3)

Ex 11 — Simplify: (x² + 7x + 12)/(x² + 5x + 6)

Factor: (x+4)(x+3)/[(x+2)(x+3)]

Cancel (x+3): (x+4)/(x+2),   x ≠ −3, −2

Ex 12 — Divide: [(x²+2x−8)/(x²−4)] ÷ [(x+4)/(x−2)]

= (x+4)(x−2)/[(x+2)(x−2)] · (x−2)/(x+4)

Cancel: (x−2)/(x+2)

6.4 Adding and Subtracting Rational Expressions

Same Denominator

a/c ± b/c = (a ± b)/c

Add or subtract numerators; keep denominator; simplify result.

Different Denominators

1. Factor all denominators

2. Find the LCD (product of all distinct factors)

3. Rewrite each fraction with LCD

4. Add/subtract numerators; simplify

Complex fractions: multiply numerator and denominator of the main fraction by the LCD of all inner fractions.

Examples

Ex 13 — Add: 5/(x+2) + 3/(x+2)

Same denominator: (5+3)/(x+2) = 8/(x+2)

Ex 14 — Add: 4/x + 3/(x+5)

LCD = x(x+5)

[4(x+5) + 3x] / [x(x+5)] = (7x+20)/[x(x+5)]

Ex 15 — Subtract: 3/(x²−4) − 1/(x+2)

LCD = (x+2)(x−2)

[3 − (x−2)] / [(x+2)(x−2)] = (5−x)/[(x+2)(x−2)]

Ex 16 — Simplify complex fraction: (1/x − 1/4) / (1/x + 1/2)

Multiply top and bottom by 4x:

(4 − x)/(4 + 2x) = (4−x)/[2(2+x)]

6.5 Solving Rational Equations

Methods

Cross Multiply (when equation = two fractions):

a/b = c/d  ⟹  ad = bc

LCD Method: multiply every term by the LCD to clear all denominators, then solve the resulting polynomial equation.

⚠ Always check for extraneous solutions — values that make any original denominator equal zero.

Inverse of a Function

To find f⁻¹(x): swap x and y, then solve for y.

f(f⁻¹(x)) = x   and   f⁻¹(f(x)) = x

For rational functions, check that the domain of f⁻¹ excludes values that create denominators of zero.

Examples

Ex 17 — Solve: 3/x = 12/20

Cross multiply: 60 = 12x → x = 5

Ex 18 — Solve: 4/(x−2) + 1 = 6/(x−2)

LCD = (x−2); multiply through: 4 + (x−2) = 6

x + 2 = 6 → x = 4   Check: x ≠ 2 ✓

Ex 19 — Solve: x/(x−3) = 3/(x−3) + 5. Check for extraneous solutions.

Multiply by (x−3): x = 3 + 5(x−3) = 5x−12 → −4x = −12 → x = 3

x = 3 makes denominator zero → No solution (extraneous)

Ex 20 — Find the inverse of f(x) = (2x+3)/(x−1)

Swap: x = (2y+3)/(y−1) → x(y−1) = 2y+3 → xy − x = 2y + 3

y(x−2) = x+3 → f⁻¹(x) = (x+3)/(x−2), x ≠ 2
