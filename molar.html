4. Step-by-Step Conversion Lab

Example 1: Mass to Particles (The Jewelry Shop)
Problem: You have a 19.7 gram gold (Au) ring. How many gold atoms are in it?
Step 1: Go to the Hub. Divide by Molar Mass of Gold (197 g/mol).
19.7 g ÷ 197 g/mol = 0.1 Moles
Step 2: Go to Particles. Multiply by 6.022 x 10²³.
0.1 mol × (6.022 x 10²³) = 6.022 x 10²² Atoms
Example 2: Molarity to Mass (The Medicine Cabinet)
Problem: You need to make 0.5 Liters of a 2.0 M Sugar (C₆H₁₂O₆) solution. How many grams of sugar do you need?
Step 1: Find the Moles needed. Multiply Molarity by Liters.
2.0 M × 0.5 L = 1.0 Mole
Step 2: Convert Moles to Grams. Multiply by Molar Mass of Sugar (180 g/mol).
1.0 mol × 180 g/mol = 180 Grams
Example 3: Particles to Molarity (The Water Treatment Lab)
Problem: A 2.0 Liter sample of water contains 3.011 x 10²³ molecules of a pollutant. What is the Molarity?
Step 1: Go to the Hub. Divide by 6.022 x 10²³.
(3.011 x 10²³) ÷ (6.022 x 10²³) = 0.5 Moles
Step 2: Find Molarity. Divide Moles by Volume (2.0 L).
0.5 mol ÷ 2.0 L = 0.25 M
