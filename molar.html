Avogadro, Molar Mass, Molarity, & Dilution

1. The Scale: Avogadro's Number

A Mole is just a number ($6.022 \times 10^{23}$). It's how we group tiny atoms into a "truckload" we can actually weigh.

Notice the Logarithmic scale: A mole isn't just bigger; it's astronomically bigger than anything we use in daily life.

2. Molar Mass: Identity Matters

One mole of different elements has the same count but different weights. Think of a dozen eggs vs. a dozen bowling balls.

Mass (g) = Moles × Molar Mass

4. Molarity: The "Crowdedness"

Molarity (M) is how many moles are packed into 1 Liter. As you add more water (Volume), the particles spread out and Molarity drops.

Molarity (M) = Moles / Liters

5. Dilution: $M_1V_1 = M_2V_2$

When you dilute, you add water but keep the moles the same. The concentration goes down because the space goes up.

Problem: Dilute 100mL of 2.0M to 500mL.
Result: (2.0M × 0.1L) / 0.5L = 0.4M
