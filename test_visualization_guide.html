The 7 Habits I Visualize Before & During Every Test
1
Read Every Word of the Prompt
I see myself slowly reading each question, underlining key words like "NOT" or "EXCEPT." I never start writing until I truly understand what's being asked.
📖 "I read to understand, not just to finish."
2
Slow Down — No Careless Mistakes
I picture pausing one breath after each step to check signs, units, and details. I never throw away easy points I've already earned.
🎯 "Slow is smooth. Smooth is high scores."
3
Stuck? Circle It & Move Forward
When I hit a hard problem, I confidently star it and keep going. Fresh eyes later often make the answer obvious the second time around.
⏩ "Every point counts — grab the easy ones first."
4
Quick Common-Sense Check
After each answer I ask: "Does this make sense?" If I solved for age and got 300 years, I know something's wrong. I picture catching those moments every time.
🧠 "My gut is a great first editor."
5
Thorough Review — Never Turn In Early
I visualize finishing, taking a breath, then reviewing every answer carefully. I imagine catching at least one mistake. Extra time = extra points. Always.
🔍 "Finished first doesn't mean finished best."
6
Breathe Through Anxiety
When panic creeps in, I put my pencil down for 5 seconds and breathe slowly. Anxiety lies — it tells me I don't know things I actually do. A calm mind remembers more.
🌬️ "Breathe in confidence, breathe out doubt."
7
Use Process of Elimination
On multiple-choice, I cross off answers I know are wrong. Going from 4 choices to 2 makes a smart guess possible. A reasoned guess beats a blank every time.
✂️ "Eliminate the wrong. Reveal the right."
